WHAT WE STUDY
About us

The Laboratory of Microbial and Cancer Genomics,established in 2018, is committed to advancing scientific research in molecular microbiology and cancer biology. Our diverse research portfolio includes bacterial genome analysis, probiotic research, investigations into urinary tract infections, and studies on antibiotic resistance. In addition, we focus on elucidating the roles of transcription factors in colorectal cancer through the analysis of patient biopsy samples. Our work also extends to conducting comprehensive statistical analyses of risk factors associated with colorectal and other solid cancers.

Under the leadership of Professor A. M. Masudul Azad Chowdhury, Ph.D., the laboratory fosters a dynamic and collaborative research environment. The team comprises research investigators and research officer, research assistants and master’sthesis students. Together, we strive to address critical challenges in cancer genomics and infectious diseases, contributing to impactful advancements in these fields.

PROJECTS
RUNNING
Analysis of the potency of drinking water coliforms as probiotic against Salmonella typhi

This research is vital for Bangladesh, where waterborne diseases like typhoid are prevalent due to contaminated drinking water. Investigating coliforms as potential probiotics against Salmonella typhi offers a sustainable solution to improve public health. It addresses the dual challenge of ensuring water safety and combating antibiotic resistance, promoting innovative approaches to disease prevention in a resource-constrained setting.


Duration:Jul 2023 - Present


Funding source : University Grants Commission Bangladesh.

COMPLETE
Quantitative analysis of transcription Factor NRF2 in human colorectal cancer in Chattogram, Bangladesh

The transcription factor NRF2 plays a critical role in regulating cellular defense against oxidative stress and is linked to cancer progression. In colorectal cancer, its dysregulation can influence tumor growth and therapy resistance. This study focuses on quantifying NRF2 expression in colorectal cancer patients in Chattogram, Bangladesh, aiming to uncover its impact and potential as a therapeutic target.


Duration:Jan 2022 - Jun 2023


Funding source : Research and Publication Cell, CU.

Prof. A. M. Masudul Azad Chowdhury,Ph.D

Director and Principle Investigator

Professor Chowdhury received his PhD degree in Science with an emphasis on Cancer Biology from Doshisha University, Japan (2017) and the M.S. degree in Microbiology with an emphasis on Bacterial antibiotic resistance from University of Chittagong, Bangladesh (2007). He is also trained at Federation University Australia (2014) and at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory (2016), New York, USA in Applied Microbiology and Cancer Biology respectively. His academic career at the University of Chittagong spans over 15 years, starting as a Lecturer (2008), and later gradually promoted to Professor (2022). His areas of research interests include

(1) Molecular mechanisms of antibiotic resistance and transmission
(2) Understanding the functions of CNC family transcription factors in cancer development
(3) Development of probiotics as an alternative of antibiotics against bacterial infection.

Dr. Chowdhury has published one book chapter and more than 25 papers in scientific journals from various publishers including Nature, SAGE, Elsevier, PLOS, Springer, Wiley etc. His PhD research unearths the molecular regulation of one of the most important cancer driving transcription factor NRF3. He is also a passionate researcher on antibiotic resistance in Bangladesh and has published more than 20 scientific articles in this field since 2009. He won national grants from University Grants Commission (UGC) and Ministry of Science & technology Bangladesh. Professor Chowdhury is a Government Scholar of Japan (2014-2017) and also a recipient of several scholarships from Australia (2014). The prime goal of his teaching is to instill students with intrinsic motivation to learn.

Area of interest:
Molecular Genetics, Cancer Cell biology, Microbial genetics, Antibiotic Resistance.
